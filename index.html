About

I am a ThM candidate in Biblical Studies at Princeton Theological Seminary, centering my work on the New Testament. I bring close study of the ancient languages — Biblical Greek, Hebrew, and Latin — together with computational methods, building projects such as AncientLibrary.org and assembling English translations such as the Terebinth Translation. My background spans physics (Duke) and public administration (UNLV), and I have founded and built software ventures alongside my academic work.

Research & Projects

  • Terebinth English Translation of New Testament

    A "syntactical" translation that seeks to reliably convey the underlying Greek syntax in English for New Testament scholars and students of biblical Greek.

  • Ancient Library

    Assembled and encoded a multilingual corpus of ancient sacred and biblically-adjacent texts (Hebrew, Greek, Latin), with full morphological tagging, lemma-based concordance search, cross-reference linking, and original-plus-translation parallel editions.
